🕌 Charminar
🕰️ History

Charminar was built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the founding of Hyderabad and possibly to mark the end of a deadly plague. It soon became the symbolic center of the city.

📍 Location

Located in the heart of Hyderabad, surrounded by historic bazaars and vibrant streets.

🏛️ Architecture

Square monument with four grand arches facing cardinal directions

Four minarets, each about 48 meters tall

Indo-Islamic style with Persian influences

Spiral staircases inside minarets lead to the upper floor

Small mosque located on the top level

✨ Interesting Facts

“Charminar” literally means Four Minarets

Acts as Hyderabad’s most recognizable landmark

Laad Bazaar nearby is famous for bangles and pearls

🧭 Visiting Info

Open daily, roughly 9:30 AM – 5:30 PM

Small entry fee

Evening visits offer beautiful city views
