🕰️ History
The Ajanta Caves were created between the 2nd century BCE and about the 6th century CE.
They were built mainly by Buddhist monks as monasteries (viharas) and prayer halls (chaityas).
The caves were forgotten for centuries and rediscovered in 1819 during British rule. Today, they are protected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

📍 Location
Ajanta Caves are located near Aurangabad in Maharashtra, India.
They lie in a horseshoe-shaped gorge along the Waghora River.

🏛️ Architecture
Total of 30 rock-cut caves carved into basalt cliffs
Includes monasteries (living quarters) and prayer halls
Famous for ancient wall paintings and sculptures
Paintings depict Buddha’s life and Jataka tales (stories of Buddha’s previous births)
Uses natural pigments and shows advanced understanding of light, shade, and perspective
The caves are considered masterpieces of Indian Buddhist art.

✨ Interesting Facts
The murals are over 1,500 years old and still retain color.
Ajanta represents one of the finest surviving examples of ancient Indian painting.
Entire caves were carved by hand using simple tools.
Designed so sunlight naturally illuminates the interiors.
