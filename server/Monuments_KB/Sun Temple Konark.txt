Sun Temple Konark
🕰️ History

The Sun Temple at Konark was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty. It was dedicated to Surya, the Sun God.

Over time, parts of the temple collapsed due to natural causes and invasions, but its remaining structures still reflect its former glory. It is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

📍 Location

The temple is located in Konark, near the Bay of Bengal in Odisha.

🏛️ Architecture

Designed as a gigantic stone chariot of the Sun God

Features 24 carved wheels and 7 stone horses

Built in Kalinga architectural style

Walls covered with detailed sculptures of gods, dancers, animals, and daily life

Main tower originally stood about 70 meters high

The wheels also function as sundials, showing accurate time through shadows.

✨ Interesting Facts

Often called the Black Pagoda by early European sailors

Temple wheels can tell time

Masterpiece of ancient Indian stone engineering

Hosts the annual Konark Dance Festival

One of India’s finest examples of temple sculpture
