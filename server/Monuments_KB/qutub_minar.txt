Qutub Minar
🕰️ History

Qutub Minar was started in 1199 CE by Qutbuddin Aibak and later completed by his successor Iltutmish. Additional repairs and restorations were carried out by later rulers, including Firoz Shah Tughlaq.

It was built to celebrate Muslim rule in Delhi and also served as a minaret for the nearby mosque. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India’s tallest historic towers.

📍 Location

Qutub Minar is located in New Delhi, inside the Qutub Complex.

🏛️ Architecture

Height: about 73 meters

Made of red sandstone and marble

Five storeys with projecting balconies

Decorated with Quranic inscriptions and geometric designs

Tapers as it rises, giving it an elegant appearance

Surrounded by important structures like Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and Iron Pillar

It represents early Indo-Islamic architecture in India.

✨ Interesting Facts

Tallest brick minaret in the world

Leaning slightly due to age and earthquakes

Part of a large historical complex with monuments from different eras

Names of builders are carved on its walls

Visible from several parts of South Delhi
