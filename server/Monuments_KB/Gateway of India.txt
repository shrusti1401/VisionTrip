Gateway of India
🕰️ History

The Gateway of India was built to commemorate the visit of George V and Mary of Teck to India in 1911. Construction began in 1913 and the monument was completed in 1924.

Ironically, while it was originally meant to symbolize British imperial power, the Gateway later became a symbol of Indian independence — in 1948, the last British troops departed India through this very archway.

Today, it stands as one of Mumbai’s most recognizable landmarks and an important reminder of India’s colonial past.

📍 Location

The Gateway of India is located on the waterfront in Mumbai, Maharashtra, overlooking the Arabian Sea.
It stands opposite the famous Taj Mahal Palace Hotel in the Colaba area.

🏛️ Architecture

Built in Indo-Saracenic architectural style (blend of Indian, Islamic, and European elements)

Constructed using yellow basalt stone with reinforced concrete

Central arch is about 26 meters (85 feet) high

Features intricate latticework and decorative motifs inspired by Gujarati architecture

Large open plaza in front, often used as a gathering space

The design was created by architect George Wittet.

✨ Interesting Facts

It was once the ceremonial entrance point for British governors and viceroys arriving by sea.

The last British soldiers left India through this monument in 1948.

Boats to Elephanta Caves depart from nearby.

It is one of the most photographed monuments in India.

The monument looks especially beautiful when illuminated at night.

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: 24 hours (outdoor monument)

Entry: Free

Best time to visit: Early morning or evening

Best season: October to March

Nearby boat rides available to Elephanta Caves

Expect crowds, especially on weekends and holidays
