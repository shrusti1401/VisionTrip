Basilica of Bom Jesus
🕰️ History

Completed in 1605 during Portuguese colonial rule, the Basilica of Bom Jesus is one of India’s most important Christian monuments. It is especially famous for housing the preserved mortal remains of Francis Xavier, a key figure in spreading Christianity in Asia. The church is part of the UNESCO-listed Churches and Convents of Goa.

📍 Location

Situated in Old Goa, once the capital of Portuguese India.

🏛️ Architecture

Built in Baroque architectural style

Exterior constructed with laterite stone

Interior richly decorated with gilded wooden altars

Main altar dedicated to Infant Jesus (Bom Jesus)

Tomb of St. Francis Xavier made of marble and jasper

✨ Interesting Facts

The saint’s body is displayed to the public every ten years

One of the oldest churches in India

Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site

🧭 Visiting Info

Open daily (timings vary by season)

No entry fee

Best months to visit: November to February
