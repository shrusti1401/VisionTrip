🌸 Lotus Temple
🕰️ History

The Lotus Temple was completed in 1986 and serves as India’s Baháʼí House of Worship. It was designed by Iranian-Canadian architect Fariborz Sahba.

The temple follows Baháʼí principles of unity and welcomes people of all religions, castes, and nationalities for silent prayer and meditation. Since its opening, it has become one of the most visited modern monuments in the world.

📍 Location

The Lotus Temple is located in New Delhi, near Kalkaji in South Delhi.

🏛️ Architecture

Shaped like a lotus flower with 27 white marble petals

Arranged in clusters of three to form nine sides (nine symbolizes completeness in Baháʼí faith)

Built using marble from Greece

Surrounded by nine reflecting pools and landscaped gardens

Central prayer hall can hold about 2,500 people

No idols, pictures, or rituals inside — only quiet contemplation

Its striking modern design blends symbolism with simplicity.

✨ Interesting Facts

One of the most visited buildings in the world

Open to followers of every religion

Appears pinkish at sunrise and glowing white at noon

Won several international architecture awards

Known for its peaceful atmosphere despite being in a busy city

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: Tuesday to Sunday (closed Monday)

Entry: Free

Best time: Morning or late afternoon

Best season: October to March

Silence must be maintained inside
