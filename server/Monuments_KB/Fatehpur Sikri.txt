Fatehpur Sikri
🕰️ History

Fatehpur Sikri was founded in 1571 by Mughal Emperor Akbar and served as the capital of the Mughal Empire for about 14 years.

Akbar built this city after the Sufi saint Salim Chishti foretold the birth of his son. In gratitude, Akbar constructed a grand city around the saint’s shrine.

Despite its architectural brilliance, Fatehpur Sikri was abandoned mainly due to shortage of water and strategic reasons. Today, it stands as a remarkably preserved example of Mughal urban planning and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

📍 Location

Fatehpur Sikri is located about 40 km west of Agra in Uttar Pradesh, India.
It sits on a rocky ridge overlooking the surrounding plains.

🏛️ Architecture

Fatehpur Sikri is built mainly of red sandstone and showcases a unique blend of Islamic, Persian, and Hindu architectural styles.

Major structures include:

Buland Darwaza – A massive victory gateway, among the tallest in India

Jama Masjid – One of the largest mosques of its time

Tomb of Salim Chishti – A white marble shrine with delicate lattice screens

Diwan-i-Khas – Hall of private audience with a famous central pillar

Panch Mahal – Five-storey palace designed for ventilation and relaxation

Jodha Bai’s Palace – Residential quarters reflecting Hindu design elements

The city was carefully planned with palaces, courtyards, audience halls, and religious buildings arranged around open spaces.

✨ Interesting Facts

Fatehpur Sikri is often called a “ghost city” because it was abandoned soon after completion.

The marble tomb of Salim Chishti is still visited by devotees who tie threads on its screens to make wishes.

Buland Darwaza stands about 54 meters high.

The entire complex was built in less than two decades.

It is one of the best-preserved Mughal capitals in India.

🧭 Visiting Info
