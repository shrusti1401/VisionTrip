Jamali Kamali Tomb
🕰️ History

Jamali Kamali Tomb dates back to the early 16th century and is associated with the Sufi saint and poet Jamali and his companion Kamali.

Jamali was a respected spiritual figure during the reign of Sikandar Lodi and later Babur. After Jamali’s death, this tomb was built to honor both Jamali and Kamali, making it a rare double mausoleum.

📍 Location

The tomb is located in the Mehrauli area of New Delhi, inside the Mehrauli Archaeological Park.

🏛️ Architecture

Built in Mughal-era Indo-Islamic style

Exterior made of red sandstone and marble

Interior richly decorated with colored tiles, Quranic calligraphy, and geometric patterns

Square chamber topped with a dome

Two graves placed side by side inside

Though small in size, the tomb is admired for its elegant interior decoration and peaceful surroundings.

✨ Interesting Facts

One of Delhi’s lesser-known heritage monuments

Considered among the earliest Mughal tombs in the city

Popular in local folklore as a “haunted” site (though historically it’s simply a quiet, isolated monument)

Surrounded by lush greenery inside Mehrauli Archaeological Park
