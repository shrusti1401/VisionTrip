Charar-e-Sharif
🕰️ History

Charar-e-Sharif is one of the most important Sufi shrines in Kashmir. It is dedicated to Sheikh Noor-ud-din Wali (also known as Nund Rishi), a revered 14th–15th century mystic and poet who founded the Rishi order of Sufism in Kashmir.

The shrine marks the place where the saint lived and later attained samadhi. Over centuries, it became a major spiritual center promoting harmony, simplicity, and communal unity.
The original wooden structure was tragically destroyed by fire in 1995 during regional unrest, but it was later carefully rebuilt using traditional Kashmiri architectural methods.

Today, Charar-e-Sharif stands as a symbol of resilience and spiritual heritage.

📍 Location

The shrine is located in the town of Charar-e-Sharif in Budgam district, about 30 km southwest of Srinagar, in Jammu & Kashmir, India.
It lies amid scenic hills and valleys, adding to its peaceful atmosphere.

🏛️ Architecture

Built mainly of wood in traditional Kashmiri style

Distinct multi-tiered pyramidal roof

Intricate wooden carvings and latticework

Central prayer hall with the saint’s tomb at its heart

Design reflects simplicity, matching the spiritual philosophy of Sheikh Noor-ud-din Wali

The rebuilt shrine closely follows the original historical layout and craftsmanship.

✨ Interesting Facts

Sheikh Noor-ud-din Wali preached unity between Hindu and Muslim communities.

He is regarded as Kashmir’s patron saint.

The shrine attracts devotees of all faiths.

His poetry, written in Kashmiri, still influences local culture.

The shrine’s reconstruction after 1995 is considered a major cultural restoration effort.

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: Daily (daytime hours are best)

Entry: Free

Best time to visit: April to October (pleasant weather)

Major annual urs (death anniversary) attracts thousands of pilgrims

Modest dress and respectful behavior are expected
