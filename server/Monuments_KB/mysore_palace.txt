Mysore Palace
🕰️ History

Mysore Palace, also called Amba Vilas Palace, was the royal residence of the Wadiyar dynasty.

The present structure was completed in 1912 after the old palace was destroyed by fire. It was commissioned by Maharaja Krishnaraja Wadiyar IV and designed by British architect Henry Irwin.

Today, it stands as one of India’s grandest palaces and a symbol of Mysore’s royal heritage.

📍 Location

Mysore Palace is situated in the center of Mysuru (formerly Mysore), Karnataka.

🏛️ Architecture

Built in Indo-Saracenic style (blend of Hindu, Muslim, Rajput, and Gothic elements)

Three-storey structure with marble domes and a towering central arch

Lavish interiors with stained glass ceilings, carved doors, and mosaic floors

Large Durbar Hall and Kalyana Mantapa (wedding hall)

Surrounded by expansive gardens

At night, nearly 100,000 bulbs illuminate the palace, creating a breathtaking view.

✨ Interesting Facts

One of the most visited monuments in India after the Taj Mahal

Still partly used by the royal family

Famous for Dasara festival celebrations held every year

Displays royal costumes, weapons, paintings, and artifacts

Palace illumination on Sundays and holidays is a major attraction

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: Daily, usually 10:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Entry: Ticket required

Illumination: Sundays & public holidays (evening)

Best season: October to March

Audio guides available
