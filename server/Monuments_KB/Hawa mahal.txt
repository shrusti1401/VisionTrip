Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)
🕰️ History

Hawa Mahal was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the grandson of Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II (founder of Jaipur). The palace was designed by Lal Chand Ustad.

It was constructed mainly for the royal women of the Rajput court. According to the purdah system, women were not allowed to appear in public, so Hawa Mahal allowed them to observe street festivals and daily life through its many screened windows—without being seen.

📍 Location

Hawa Mahal is located in the heart of Jaipur, Rajasthan, near the City Palace and bustling old bazaars.

🏛️ Architecture

Built from red and pink sandstone (matching Jaipur’s “Pink City” theme)

Five-storey pyramidal façade shaped like Lord Krishna’s crown

Features 953 small jharokha windows with intricate latticework

Designed for natural ventilation — cool air flows through the windows, giving it the name Palace of Winds

No main foundation; the structure curves slightly to support its height

Rear side contains courtyards, chambers, and a small museum

Architectural style is a blend of Rajput and Mughal elements, with floral motifs and delicate carvings.

✨ Interesting Facts

Despite its grand façade, Hawa Mahal is quite narrow from inside.

It was never meant to be a royal residence — mainly a viewing gallery.

The many windows create a natural cooling system, even in Jaipur’s hot summers.

The palace looks most beautiful in early morning sunlight.

It is one of Jaipur’s most photographed landmarks.

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: Daily, usually 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Entry: Ticket required

Best time to visit: Early morning or late afternoon

Best season: October to March

Small museum inside displays royal artifacts
