Brihadeeswarar Temple

(also called Thanjavur / Tanjavur Big Temple)

🕰️ History

The Brihadeeswarar Temple was built around 1010 CE by the great Chola ruler Rajaraja Chola I. It was dedicated to Lord Shiva and served as a major religious and cultural center of the Chola Empire.

The temple reflects the political power, artistic excellence, and advanced engineering of the Chola dynasty. It is now part of UNESCO’s “Great Living Chola Temples” and continues to function as an active place of worship.

📍 Location

The temple is located in Thanjavur, in Tamil Nadu.

🏛️ Architecture

Built entirely of granite (even though granite is not locally available)

Main tower (Vimana) rises about 66 meters, one of the tallest temple towers in India

Massive stone Shiva Lingam inside the sanctum

Huge Nandi (bull) statue carved from a single stone

Temple complex includes pillared halls, shrines, and murals

A remarkable feature is the 80-ton capstone placed on top of the tower using a long sloping ramp — an incredible engineering feat for its time.

✨ Interesting Facts

Built more than 1,000 years ago and still structurally strong

Shadow of the main tower reportedly does not fall on the ground at noon

One of the finest examples of Dravidian temple architecture

Inscriptions on walls record donations, dancers, musicians, and daily rituals

Still an active temple with regular prayers

🧭 Visiting Info

Open: Daily (morning and evening hours)

Entry: Free

Best season: October to March

Best time: Early morning or evening

Modest clothing recommended

Photography allowed in outer areas
