India Gate
🕰️ History

India Gate was built as a war memorial to honor more than 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Indian Army during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.

The monument was designed by British architect Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931. Originally called the All India War Memorial, it later became known as India Gate after independence.

In 1971, following the Indo-Pak war, the Amar Jawan Jyoti (Eternal Flame of the Immortal Soldier) was added beneath the arch to honor India’s unknown soldiers. India Gate today stands as a powerful symbol of sacrifice, bravery, and national pride.

📍 Location

India Gate is located in the heart of New Delhi, at the eastern end of Rajpath (now Kartavya Path).
It is surrounded by large green lawns and government buildings, making it a popular public gathering place.

🏛️ Architecture

Built in the style of a triumphal arch

Constructed using sandstone and granite

Stands about 42 meters high

Names of thousands of fallen soldiers are inscribed on its walls

Broad archway with a shallow dome on top

Simple yet imposing design reflecting dignity and remembrance

Its structure is inspired by European war memorials but adapted to Indian materials and surroundings.

✨ Interesting Facts

Over 13,000 names of soldiers are engraved on the monument.

Amar Jawan Jyoti burned continuously for decades as a tribute to martyrs.

India Gate looks especially beautiful when illuminated at night.

It is one of Delhi’s most visited landmarks.

The lawns around India Gate are a favorite picnic spot for locals and tourists.
