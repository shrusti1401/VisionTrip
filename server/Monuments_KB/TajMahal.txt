History
The Taj Mahal was built between 1632 and 1653 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.
It serves as her mausoleum and is considered a powerful symbol of eternal love.

Location
The monument is located in Agra, on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in Uttar Pradesh, India.

Architecture
Built mainly from white Makrana marble
Perfect symmetry in design (gardens, minarets, and main dome)
Large central dome surrounded by four slender minarets
Intricate inlay work using semi-precious stones (called pietra dura)
Combines Islamic, Persian, and Indian architectural styles
The entire complex includes a mosque, guest house, formal gardens, and reflecting pools.

Interesting Facts
The color of the Taj Mahal appears to change during the day (pinkish in morning, white at noon, golden at night).
Over 20,000 artisans worked on it.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Both Mumtaz Mahal and Shah Jahan are buried inside.
The minarets are slightly tilted outward so they won’t fall on the tomb during earthquakes.

Visiting Info
Opening hours: Sunrise to sunset (closed on Fridays)
Best time to visit: Early morning or late afternoon
Recommended season: October to March (pleasant weather)
Entry: Tickets available online and at counters
Night viewing is allowed on full-moon nights (with special tickets)
