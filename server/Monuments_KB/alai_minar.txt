Alai Minar
🕰️ History

Alai Minar was commissioned in 1311 CE by Sultan Alauddin Khalji. It was intended to be a grand victory tower and was planned to rise twice the height of Qutub Minar, symbolizing Khalji’s power and expansion of the Delhi Sultanate. However, after Alauddin Khalji’s death in 1316, construction was abandoned, leaving only the massive base completed.

📍 Location

Alai Minar stands within the Qutub Complex in New Delhi, alongside other historic structures from different periods of medieval India.

🏛️ Architecture

Circular foundation made from rubble masonry

Diameter of about 25 meters, indicating its intended enormous height

Rough stone exterior, unlike the refined carving of nearby monuments

Designed as a towering minaret but left incomplete

✨ Interesting Facts

If finished, it would have been the tallest minaret in India

Represents one of the most ambitious but unrealized architectural projects of the Delhi Sultanate

Today, only the first storey remains as a reminder of Khalji’s grand vision

🧭 Visiting Info

Open daily from sunrise to sunset

Entry covered under Qutub Complex ticket

Best visited in early morning or late afternoon
