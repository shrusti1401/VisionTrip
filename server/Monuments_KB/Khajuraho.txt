Khajuraho Group of Monuments
🕰️ History

The Khajuraho temples were built between 950 and 1050 CE by the Chandela dynasty.

Originally, there were about 85 temples, but only around 25 survive today. These temples were forgotten for centuries and rediscovered in the 19th century. Khajuraho is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is world-famous for its artistic stone carvings.

📍 Location

Khajuraho is located in Khajuraho, in Madhya Pradesh.

🏛️ Architecture

The temples are divided into three groups: Western, Eastern, and Southern.

Key architectural features:

Built in Nagara (North Indian) style

Raised stone platforms with steep staircases

Tall spires (shikharas) symbolizing Mount Kailash

Walls covered with detailed sculptures of gods, goddesses, dancers, musicians, daily life scenes, and famous erotic figures

No mortar used — stones fitted together with precision

Most temples are dedicated to Hindu deities like Shiva and Vishnu, while some belong to Jain tradition.

✨ Interesting Facts

Only about 10% of the sculptures are erotic — the rest show everyday life and spirituality

Temples reflect a balance between physical life and spiritual devotion

Carvings are so detailed that jewelry, expressions, and movement appear lifelike

Khajuraho hosts an annual dance festival celebrating classical Indian dance

Considered one of India’s greatest artistic achievements in stone
