Victoria Memorial
🕰️ History

Victoria Memorial was built between 1906 and 1921 in memory of Queen Victoria after her death. It was designed by British architect William Emerson and served as a symbol of British imperial power in India.

Today, it functions as a museum showcasing colonial history, paintings, manuscripts, and artifacts from the British period.

📍 Location

The memorial stands in Kolkata, surrounded by large landscaped gardens.

🏛️ Architecture

Built entirely of white Makrana marble

Indo-Saracenic style (blend of British, Mughal, Venetian, and Egyptian elements)

Large central dome with smaller domes around

Statue of Victory on top of the dome

Grand halls, galleries, and arched verandas

The interior houses galleries displaying royal portraits, weapons, letters, and historical exhibits.

✨ Interesting Facts

One of the largest marble buildings in India

Gardens cover about 64 acres

Evening light-and-sound show narrates Kolkata’s history

Popular picnic and photography spot

Contains over 25 galleries
