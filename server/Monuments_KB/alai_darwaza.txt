🕌 Alai Darwaza
🕰️ History

Alai Darwaza was built in 1311 CE by Alauddin Khalji.
It served as the southern gateway to the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque complex and marks an important phase of Indo-Islamic architecture.

📍 Location

Situated inside the Qutub Complex in New Delhi, India.

🏛️ Architecture

Built using red sandstone and white marble

First building in India to use true Islamic arches and domes

Decorated with geometric patterns and Quranic inscriptions

Square structure with arched entrances on three sides

✨ Interesting Facts

Considered the first true Islamic gateway in India

Features advanced dome engineering for its time

Designed as part of Alauddin Khalji’s grand expansion plans

🧭 Visiting Info

Open daily: Sunrise to sunset

Entry included with Qutub Complex ticket

Best time to visit: Morning or late afternoon

Easily accessible by metro and local transport
